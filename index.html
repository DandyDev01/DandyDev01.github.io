Artist Statement

My journey began in 2016 when I learned about Unity from an online ad for a course on Udemy by GameDev.tv. I had always wanted to make games and learn to program; my discovery afforded me the opportunity to do both. I started out designing small games, but this quickly morphed into doing interaction design for games and other interactive systems. My focus shifted from making games that were fun to play to being making games that felt good to play while still being fun. At this time, I also started making of types of software outside of games like a character manager for tabletop RPGs. The focus of my work is to make interactive systems that are simple to use and as accessible as I can manage with the technologies I have available to me. I believe in giving users multiple ways to complete tasks as this will not only help with accessibility but also supports a range of different types of users. Having more than one way to complete a task means novice users can have an easy-to-follow method, but power users can also be accommodated with short-cuts and other features that are quicker than the step by step process a new user would follow. For me creating simple to use interactive systems means breaking things down to the bare minimum and discarding anything that is not relevant to the current context of the system state. This lessons the cognitive load on the end user, making things easier for new users.
